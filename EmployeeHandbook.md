# Employee Handbook

## 1. Welcome and Introduction
### 1.1 Welcome Message
Welcome to TechCorp Solutions! This handbook outlines our company policies, procedures, and benefits. Please read it carefully and contact HR with questions.

### 1.2 Company Mission and Values
**Our Mission:** To empower businesses through innovative technology solutions.

**Our Core Values:** Innovation, Integrity, Excellence, Collaboration, Client Focus, Continuous Learning, Work-Life Balance.

### 1.3 Company History
Founded in 2010, TechCorp Solutions has grown from a 5-person startup to a 500+ employee technology consulting firm serving 300+ enterprise clients globally across eight offices in North America and Europe.

## 2. Employment Policies
### 2.1 Equal Employment Opportunity
TechCorp Solutions is an Equal Opportunity Employer committed to a diverse and inclusive workplace. We prohibit discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, marital status, veteran status, or other protected characteristics. This applies to all employment decisions including hiring, compensation, training, promotions, and termination.

Report discrimination to your supervisor, HR, or our ethics hotline (1-800-XXX-XXXX). All complaints will be investigated. Retaliation is prohibited.

### 2.2 At-Will Employment
Employment is at-will. Either party may terminate the relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice. Only written agreements signed by the CEO or CHRO can modify this. This handbook is not a contract and policies may change without notice.

### 2.3 Employment Classifications
**Full-Time:** 40+ hours/week, eligible for all benefits
**Part-Time:** <40 hours/week, pro-rated benefits
**Temporary:** Project-based, limited duration, generally no benefits
**Exempt:** Salaried, no overtime pay
**Non-Exempt:** Hourly, eligible for overtime (1.5x after 40 hours/week)
**Independent Contractors:** Not employees, not eligible for benefits

Your classification is in your offer letter. Contact HR with questions.

### 2.4 Background Checks and Drug Testing
**Background Checks:** All final candidates undergo background checks including employment/education verification, criminal history, and other relevant checks. Conducted per FCRA guidelines. Adverse findings are individually assessed and may not automatically disqualify candidates.

**Drug Testing:** Required for safety-sensitive positions and certain client sites. May include pre-employment, random, post-accident, and reasonable suspicion testing. Conducted by certified labs. Refusal or positive results may result in offer withdrawal or termination. Drug-free workplace policy applies to all employees.

## 3. Workplace Standards
### 3.1 Code of Conduct
All employees must conduct themselves professionally and ethically. Key requirements:

**Professionalism:** Treat colleagues, clients, and visitors with respect. Communicate professionally, meet commitments, and maintain a positive attitude.

**Ethics:** Be honest and transparent. Avoid conflicts of interest. Do not accept gifts over $100. Disclose outside employment and financial interests in competitors/clients. Protect confidential information and proprietary data.

**Compliance:** Follow all laws, regulations, and company policies. Record financial transactions accurately. Use company resources appropriately. Limited personal use is acceptable if it doesn't interfere with work.

**Prohibited Conduct:** Harassment, discrimination, violence, threats, theft, fraud, substance abuse, insubordination, and unauthorized disclosure of confidential information are strictly prohibited and may result in termination.

**Reporting Violations:** Report concerns to your supervisor, HR, Legal, or the Ethics Hotline (1-800-XXX-XXXX, available 24/7). No retaliation for good faith reports.

### 3.2 Dress Code
TechCorp maintains business casual dress standards. Appropriate attire includes slacks, collared shirts, blouses, skirts (knee-length+), and professional footwear. Dark jeans in good condition are acceptable. 

**Client-facing roles** require business professional attire for meetings and presentations.

**Inappropriate:** Ripped clothing, shorts (except designated areas), tank tops, offensive graphics, athletic wear (except company-branded), flip-flops, excessively revealing clothing.

**Casual Fridays:** Jeans and company-branded casual wear permitted.

**Accommodations:** Religious, cultural, and medical dress needs will be accommodated. Contact HR to discuss.

Managers may ask employees not in compliance to change. Repeated violations may result in discipline.

### 3.3 Attendance and Punctuality
**Work Schedule:** Full-time: 40 hours/week, typically Monday-Friday. Standard hours: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM with 1-hour lunch. Flexible arrangements available with manager approval.

**Punctuality:** Arrive ready to work at your scheduled start time. Notify supervisor immediately if you'll be late. Occasional tardiness is understood, but excessive tardiness (3+ times/month) may result in discipline.

**Absences:** Notify supervisor before your scheduled start time if you cannot work. Submit vacation requests via HR portal with at least 2 weeks notice.

**No Call/No Show:** Failure to report for work and notify supervisor may result in disciplinary action. Three consecutive days of no call/no show is considered job abandonment.

**Breaks (Non-Exempt):** 30-60 minute unpaid meal break for 6+ hour shifts. Two 15-minute paid rest breaks per 8-hour shift.

**Attendance Issues:** Excessive absenteeism or tardiness will result in progressive discipline: verbal warning → written warning → final warning → termination. Absences for FMLA, approved leave, or company closures don't count against attendance.

### 3.4 Workplace Safety
TechCorp is committed to a safe work environment. All employees must follow safety rules and report hazards immediately.

**Responsibilities:** Follow safety procedures, use required PPE, report unsafe conditions and all injuries (even minor ones), participate in safety training.

**Common Hazards:** Slips/trips/falls, ergonomic issues, electrical hazards, chemical hazards. Report all hazards to your supervisor or Facilities.

**Injury Prevention:** Use ergonomic workstations, lift properly (bend knees, not waist, max 50 lbs without help), use step stools for high reaches.

**Accident Reporting:** Report all work injuries immediately to supervisor and complete incident report within 24 hours. For emergencies, call 911. Workers' compensation covers all work-related injuries.

**Emergency Procedures:** Know evacuation routes and assembly points. In case of fire/emergency: remain calm, exit via nearest safe route, assemble at designated location, do not re-enter until authorized. Active shooter: Run if safe, Hide if you can't run, Fight as last resort.

**Drug-Free Workplace:** Zero tolerance for substance abuse. Drug testing may be required for safety-sensitive positions, post-accident, or reasonable suspicion.

**Safety Violations:** May result in discipline up to termination. Employees may refuse unsafe work without retaliation.

## 4. Compensation and Benefits
### 4.1 Payroll and Compensation
TechCorp provides competitive compensation based on job responsibilities, skills, market data, location, and internal equity.

**Pay Schedule:**
- **Exempt (Salaried):** Semi-monthly on 15th and last day of month
- **Non-Exempt (Hourly):** Bi-weekly every other Friday
- Direct deposit required; pay stubs available electronically

**Overtime:** Non-exempt employees receive 1.5x pay for hours over 40/week. All overtime must be pre-approved.

**Performance Reviews:** Annual reviews determine merit increases (0-8% based on performance). Promotions typically receive 8-15% increases.

**Bonuses:** Eligible employees may receive annual performance bonuses (10-20% of salary), sales commissions, spot bonuses ($500-$5,000), and referral bonuses ($1,000-$2,000).

**Final Pay:** Includes all wages and unused vacation. Provided next regular payday or per state law. Sick leave is not paid out.

### 4.2 Health Insurance
TechCorp offers comprehensive health insurance to full-time employees (and part-time 30+ hours/week) starting the first day of the month after 30 days of employment.

**Medical Plans:** Three options via Aetna - PPO High (lowest deductible), PPO Standard (balanced), and HDHP with HSA (high deductible, HSA-eligible). TechCorp pays 80-90% of premiums. Monthly employee premiums range from $50-$295 depending on plan and coverage tier.

**HSA:** HDHP participants receive $1,000/$2,000 employer contribution annually and can contribute up to $4,300/$8,550 (individual/family) pre-tax.

**FSA:** Healthcare FSA ($3,200 max) and Dependent Care FSA ($5,000 max) available with PPO plans.

**Dental:** PPO (TechCorp pays 80%) or HMO (TechCorp pays 100%). Preventive covered 100%.

**Vision:** VSP coverage, 100% employer-paid. $10 exam, $25 lenses, $130 frame allowance annually.

**Life Insurance:** Company-paid basic life (1x salary, max $250k) plus optional supplemental coverage available.

**Disability:** Company-paid STD (60% salary, up to 26 weeks) and LTD (60% salary to age 65). Optional supplemental available.

**Enrollment:** New hires enroll within 30 days. Annual Open Enrollment in November. Qualifying life events allow mid-year changes.

**EAP:** Free confidential counseling (6 sessions/year), financial/legal consultation, work-life services available to all employees and household members.

### 4.3 Retirement Plans
TechCorp offers a 401(k) plan through Fidelity to help employees save for retirement.

**Eligibility:** All full-time and part-time (20+ hours/week) employees, eligible immediately upon hire.

**Contributions:** Contribute up to $23,500/year ($31,000 if age 50+) via pre-tax or Roth payroll deduction. Change amount anytime.

**Employer Match:** 100% match on first 3% of salary + 50% match on next 2% = up to 4% total match. Deposited each pay period.

**Vesting:** Employer match vests over 5 years (0% year 1, 25% year 2, 50% year 3, 75% year 4, 100% year 5). Employee contributions always 100% vested.

**Investment Options:** Target date funds, index funds, actively managed funds, stable value fund, company stock (max 10% recommended), and self-directed brokerage.

**Auto-Enrollment:** New hires automatically enrolled at 3% after 90 days with automatic 1% annual increase (opt-out available).

**Resources:** Free financial advisor consultations, online tools, quarterly education workshops.

**Loans & Withdrawals:** Loans available (up to $50,000 or 50% of vested balance). Hardship withdrawals permitted for qualifying events. Penalty-free withdrawals at age 59½.

**Questions:** Contact HR (benefits@techcorpsolutions.com) or Fidelity (1-800-XXX-XXXX).

**Vacation:** 15-30 days/year based on tenure (accrues monthly, rolls over, paid out at separation)
**Sick Leave:** 12 days/year for all full-time employees (not paid out)

**Bereavement:** 5 days (immediate family), 3 days (extended family)

## 5. Time Off and Leaves
### 5.1 Vacation Policy
Full-time employees accrue vacation based on tenure:
- **Years 0-3:** 15 days/year (10 hrs/month)
- **Years 4-7:** 20 days/year (13.33 hrs/month)
- **Years 8-12:** 25 days/year (16.67 hrs/month)
- **Years 13+:** 30 days/year (20 hrs/month)

Vacation accrues monthly starting day one. Maximum accrual cap is 1.5x annual amount. Request vacation at least 2 weeks in advance (4 weeks for 4+ consecutive days). Unused vacation pays out at separation (except termination for cause). Part-time employees receive pro-rated accrual.

### 5.2 Sick Leave
All full-time employees accrue 8 hours/month (12 days/96 hours per year). Maximum accrual: 480 hours.

**Uses:** Personal illness/injury, family care, medical appointments, public health emergencies, or as victim of domestic violence.

**Notification:** Contact supervisor ASAP before start time. Medical documentation required for absences 4+ consecutive days. Unused sick leave is NOT paid out at separation.

### 5.3 Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)
Eligible employees (12+ months employment, 1,250+ hours worked) may take up to 12 weeks unpaid, job-protected leave for: birth/adoption, employee's serious health condition, care for family member's serious health condition, military family leave. Military caregiver leave: up to 26 weeks.

**Process:** Provide 30 days notice when foreseeable. Submit FMLA Request Form and medical certification to HR. Health insurance continues; employee pays premiums. Must use accrued sick/vacation concurrently with FMLA. Return to same or equivalent position.

### 5.4 Parental Leave
Eligible after 6 months service:
- **Primary Caregiver:** 12 weeks paid (100% salary)
- **Secondary Caregiver:** 6 weeks paid (100% salary)
- Must be taken within 12 months of birth/placement

Birth mothers also receive 6-8 weeks STD (60% pay) for recovery before parental leave begins. All benefits continue. Gradual return options available.

### 5.5 Bereavement Leave
- **Immediate Family** (spouse, child, parent, sibling, grandparent/child): 5 days paid
- **Extended Family** (aunt, uncle, niece, nephew, in-laws): 3 days paid
- **Close Friend/Colleague:** 1 day paid
- **Pregnancy Loss:** 2 weeks paid

Use within 2 weeks of death. Documentation may be requested. EAP counseling available.

## 6. Performance and Development
### 6.1 Performance Reviews
Annual performance reviews occur in November-December. Process includes employee self-assessment, manager evaluation, and performance discussion. Ratings: Exceptional (5), Exceeds Expectations (4), Meets Expectations (3), Needs Improvement (2), Unsatisfactory (1).

Performance determines merit increases (0-8%), bonuses, and promotion eligibility. Continuous feedback encouraged through regular 1-on-1s. Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs) may be required for ratings below 3.

### 6.2 Training and Development
TechCorp invests in employee development through:
- Onboarding program (90 days with buddy system)
- TechCorp University (500+ online courses, live training, certifications)
- Leadership development programs
- Required compliance training

**Tuition Reimbursement:** Up to $5,000 (undergraduate) or $8,000 (graduate) annually. Must maintain good standing and remain employed 12 months post-completion.

**Professional Development Budget:** $1,500-$5,000/year based on level for conferences, memberships, courses, certifications.

### 6.3 Career Advancement
TechCorp promotes from within. Career tracks: Individual Contributor (Junior → Principal → Distinguished) and Management (Team Lead → VP). Internal job postings available before external recruiting. Employees may apply with manager notification (recommended).

Career development resources include IDPs, mentorship program, job rotations, and high-potential programs (Emerging Leaders, Accelerate).

### 6.4 Promotions and Transfers
**Promotions:** Based on performance, potential, skills, business need, and readiness. Typically 8-15% salary increase. Require 12+ months in current role and recent "Meets Expectations" or higher rating.

**Transfers:** Lateral moves encouraged for development. Require current manager approval (cannot be unreasonably withheld) and minimum 12 months in role. Relocation assistance available for office transfers ($5,000-full service based on level).

## 7. Technology and Data Security
### 7.1 Acceptable Use of Technology
Company technology is provided for business purposes. Limited personal use permitted if it doesn't interfere with work, consume excessive resources, or violate policies.

**Prohibited:** Offensive/harassing content, unauthorized software, circumventing security, illegal activities, excessive personal use, unauthorized cloud services. Only IT-approved software may be installed.

**Security:** Use strong unique passwords, enable MFA, lock computers when away, report lost/stolen equipment immediately. No expectation of privacy—company may monitor all use.

### 7.2 Email and Internet Policy
**Email:** Use professionally. Limited personal use permitted. Don't send spam, chain letters, large attachments without approval, or confidential info to unauthorized recipients. Beware of phishing.

**Internet:** Business use primary. Limited personal browsing during breaks. Prohibited: inappropriate content, unauthorized downloads, excessive personal use, P2P file sharing.

All activity monitored. VPN required for remote access.

### 7.3 Data Privacy and Security
Protect all confidential information: company data, client data, and personal data. Data classified as Public, Internal, Confidential, or Restricted.

**Requirements:** Access only what you need, use approved storage (no personal cloud services), encrypt sensitive data, lock files containing confidential info, shred documents, report lost devices immediately.

**Data Breach:** Report suspected breaches to IT Security immediately. Don't investigate yourself.

**Compliance:** Follow GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, PCI DSS as applicable. Violations may result in termination and legal action.

### 7.4 Social Media Policy
Personal social media use during work hours should be limited to breaks. When posting about work-related topics:
- Clearly state views are your own ("Views are my own, not my employer's")
- Never share confidential company or client information
- Be professional and respectful
- Don't speak on behalf of TechCorp without authorization
- Respect copyrights and disclosure rules

Only Marketing/Communications may post on official company accounts. Report concerning posts to HR.

## 8. Workplace Behavior and Conduct
### 8.1 Anti-Harassment Policy
TechCorp Solutions prohibits harassment of any kind. Harassment includes unwelcome conduct based on race, color, sex, gender, sexual orientation, religion, age (40+), disability, national origin, pregnancy, military status, or other protected characteristics.

**Sexual Harassment:** Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, sexual comments/jokes, inappropriate touching, or sexual images/gestures.

**Other Harassment:** Slurs, derogatory comments, offensive jokes, intimidation, hostile behavior, or exclusion based on protected characteristics.

**Reporting:** Report to your supervisor, any manager, HR (hr@techcorp.com, ext. 5500), or Ethics Hotline (1-800-XXX-XXXX, anonymous option available). All complaints investigated promptly and confidentially.

**Consequences:** Violations may result in training, warnings, suspension, or termination. **Retaliation is strictly prohibited** and will result in discipline up to termination.

### 8.2 Anti-Discrimination Policy
TechCorp Solutions provides equal employment opportunities. We do not discriminate based on protected characteristics in hiring, compensation, promotions, training, or any employment decision.

**Reasonable Accommodation:** We provide accommodations for disabilities, religious beliefs, and pregnancy-related conditions. Submit requests to HR. We'll engage in an interactive process to identify effective accommodations.

**Reporting:** Report discrimination to your supervisor, HR, or Ethics Hotline (1-800-XXX-XXXX). All complaints investigated confidentially. Retaliation prohibited.

### 8.3 Workplace Violence Prevention
**Zero Tolerance:** No violence, threats, intimidation, or weapons on company property or at work events. Prohibited: physical violence, threats (direct or indirect), weapons (except authorized security), aggressive behavior, stalking, or conduct creating fear.

**Reporting Immediate Threats:** Call 911, alert security, evacuate if safe. **Non-Immediate Concerns:** Report to supervisor, Security (ext. 5911), HR (ext. 5500), or Ethics Hotline.

**Domestic Violence:** Employees experiencing domestic violence affecting workplace safety should inform HR confidentially. We'll develop safety plans and provide support.

**Consequences:** Violations result in immediate termination, law enforcement involvement, and possible prosecution.

### 8.4 Substance Abuse Policy
**Prohibited:** Using, possessing, distributing illegal drugs, or being under the influence of alcohol/drugs during work hours or on company property. Prescription medications that impair performance must be reported to supervisor/HR.

**Alcohol at Company Events:** Moderate consumption permitted at approved events. Must arrange safe transportation. All conduct policies still apply.

**Drug/Alcohol Testing:** Conducted for pre-employment (safety-sensitive positions), reasonable suspicion, post-accident, random (safety-sensitive roles), and return-to-work. Refusal treated as positive result.

**Consequences:** First violation requires substance abuse assessment/treatment and possible suspension. Second violation or serious violations (distribution, operating equipment while impaired, refusal to test) result in termination.

**Assistance:** Contact Employee Assistance Program (1-800-555-EAP1) for confidential help. Voluntary self-reporting before violations may protect employment.

## 9. Employee Relations
### 9.1 Open Door Policy
TechCorp Solutions maintains open communication. Employees may voice concerns to:
1. Immediate supervisor
2. Department manager/director
3. Human Resources
4. Senior leadership
5. Ethics Hotline: 1-800-XXX-XXXX (anonymous option available)

All discussions handled confidentially. No retaliation against employees using this policy in good faith.

### 9.2 Grievance Procedures
**Grievances:** Formal complaints about work-related issues, including policy violations, working conditions, unfair treatment, discrimination, performance evaluations, or compensation disputes.

**Informal Resolution:** Discuss with the person involved, your supervisor, or HR before filing formal grievance.

**Formal Process:**
1. **Submit Written Grievance to HR** (within 30 days): Include details, dates, individuals involved, witnesses, policy violated, and desired resolution
2. **Investigation** (5-10 days): HR investigates and interviews parties
3. **Initial Response** (10-15 days): Written findings and action
4. **Appeal** (optional): Submit appeal to CHRO within 10 days; final decision within 15 days

All grievances handled confidentially. No retaliation.

### 9.3 Conflict Resolution
Employees encouraged to resolve conflicts through:
1. **Direct Communication:** Discuss privately, focus on behaviors not personality, use "I" statements, seek common ground
2. **Mediation:** Voluntary process with neutral HR/mediator facilitating discussion
3. **Manager Intervention:** When conflict impacts performance or involves policy violations
4. **HR Involvement:** For harassment, discrimination, retaliation, or legal concerns

Address issues promptly. Focus on problems, not people. Maintain professionalism.

### 9.4 Disciplinary Actions
**Progressive Discipline:**
1. Verbal Warning/Coaching (informal)
2. Written Warning (formal, in personnel file)
3. Final Written Warning (last chance before termination)
4. Termination

**Immediate Termination** for serious violations: theft, violence, weapons possession, harassment, discrimination, substance abuse, falsification, gross insubordination, confidentiality breaches.

**Performance Improvement Plans (PIP):** May include specific goals, timelines (30-90 days), resources, and regular check-ins. Failure to meet goals may result in termination.

## 10. Separation of Employment
### 10.1 Resignation
**Notice Requirements:** Professional/managerial employees: 4 weeks. All others: 2 weeks.

**Process:**
1. Submit written resignation to supervisor and HR (include last day)
2. Continue performing duties professionally during notice period
3. Complete/transfer pending assignments, document processes, train replacement
4. Return all company property by last day

**Final Pay:** Includes all hours worked plus accrued unused vacation (per state law), provided on next regular payday.

**Benefits:** Health insurance continues through last day of the month. COBRA and 401(k) rollover information provided by HR.

### 10.2 Termination
Employment is at-will. TechCorp Solutions may terminate at any time, with or without cause/notice.

**Types:** Voluntary (resignation, retirement, no-return from leave) or Involuntary (performance issues, policy violations, misconduct, reduction in force, position elimination).

**Progressive Discipline:** Verbal warning → Written warning → Final warning → Termination. **Immediate termination** for serious violations: theft, violence, weapons, harassment, discrimination, confidentiality breaches, substance abuse, falsification, gross insubordination.

**Process:** Termination reviewed/approved by HR and management, employee notified in private meeting, final pay/benefits discussed, company property returned immediately, system access deactivated.

### 10.3 Exit Interviews
HR conducts exit interviews during final week to gather feedback about employment experience, work environment, management, and improvement opportunities. Participation voluntary but encouraged. Responses confidential and reported in aggregate.

### 10.4 Return of Company Property
All employees must return **all** company property on/before last day:
- Electronics (laptops, tablets, phones, monitors, accessories)
- Access badges, keys, security cards
- Company credit cards
- Vehicles, tools, equipment
- Confidential documents and data (physical and electronic)
- Uniforms, manuals, materials

**Data:** Delete all company emails/documents/files from personal devices and cloud storage. Do not retain client lists or confidential information.

**Unreturned Property:** May delay final pay, cost deducted from pay where permitted, legal action may be taken. Disclosure all system passwords to IT/supervisor.
